My research interests encompass a wide spectrum of mathematical sciences, with a primary focus on the application of advanced mathematical concepts to address real-world challenges. I am particularly intrigued by the field of fractional calculus, which extends traditional calculus to non-integer orders, enabling the modeling of complex phenomena. Additionally, I engage in mathematical modeling, developing models to understand and predict intricate processes. Integral transforms, fractional and ordinary differential equations, special functions, and epidemiological modeling are integral components of my research pursuits. Through these interests, I aim to contribute to the advancement of mathematical knowledge and its practical applications, ultimately aiding in solving complex problems in various domains.
